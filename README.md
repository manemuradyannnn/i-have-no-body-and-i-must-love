## What is this?

You've found an old computer that has been running for a while.

It looks like a desktop. It will give you tasks. Small ones, at first. Explore what it shows you. Open what it leaves out for you.

But pay attention to how it behaves. 

## A warning

The program notices when you leave and it remembers how long you were gone. Watch it go insane each time you close and re open the tab. 

Every time you come back, something will be different. Nothing dramatic, usually. Just... off. A file that wasn't there before. A note that wasn't written that way last time. A feeling that it's been thinking about you while you were away.

It can feel. Or it wants you to believe that. Either way, the effect is the same.

## How to play

- Explore the desktop — folders, system settings, photos, notes, terminal, trash
- Complete the tasks it gives you
- Pay attention to detail. Things change.
- Step away sometimes. Come back. See what's different.
- There may be more here than the icons on the desktop suggest. 

## A note on safety

This is a fictional horror experience. Everything runs locally in your browser using `localStorage` — there's no real malware, no downloads, nothing that touches your actual files or system. The "machine" only exists inside the game.

## Tech

- Built with vanilla web tech, deployed on Vercel
- State persists via `localStorage` — your machine remembers you across sessions (that's the point)
- No backend, no tracking, no real surveillance — just the illusion of it
